# Flatlay Style Guide

Use the supplied fashion trend-board image as the target visual language: a white-background luxury styling board with asymmetric product collage, bilingual editorial typography, warm-gold hairline rules, and clean commercial cutout photography.

## Reference Set

- `editorial_trend_board_reference`: vertical 3:4 white canvas with a large knit top on the left, tall trousers on the right, sunglasses upper-right, handbag and shoes lower-left, captions beside items, Chinese title at upper-left, `LOOK 07` at upper-right, and small archive/curator footer text.

## Visual Rules

- Use a pure white seamless background, not a room, tabletop, colored paper, marble, wood, or textile surface.
- Use isolated product photography with a flat-lay or front-facing catalog feel. Do not show a model, hands, body parts, mannequins, hangers, or folded retail packaging.
- Use realistic garment cutouts with natural fabric wrinkles, seams, ribs, buttons, zippers, pockets, and stitching.
- Use soft grey studio shadows under each object to keep items grounded without making the image dark.
- Keep a 4K vertical 3:4 canvas by default when the user does not provide output specs. Reserve the top 18-24% for editorial whitespace and heading blocks.
- Show each item as a distinct object. Avoid piles, clutter, heavy overlap, or hard cropped edges.
- Keep styling commercial and inspectable: the user should be able to see what each item is.
- Use warm-gold hairline rules as structural accents: one under the upper-left subtitle, one under the upper-right style name, a short footer rule, and optionally a left vertical rule with tiny dot endpoints.
- Typography should feel like a luxury magazine layout: bold black Chinese title, smaller grey Chinese subtitle, uppercase English descriptor, large gold serif LOOK number, black/grey item captions.

## Layout Blueprint

- Canvas: vertical 3:4, white, airy, with more whitespace above than below.
- Header left: place a bold Chinese title near the top-left margin, a small Chinese style subtitle below it, a thin gold horizontal rule, then an uppercase English line such as `PRIVATE STYLE DIRECTION / MATERIAL EDIT`.
- Header right: place a large gold serif `LOOK 01` to `LOOK 99`, a small uppercase look name beneath it, a short thin gold rule, then a small study/editorial label.
- Body left: place the hero garment oversized across the left half, beginning below the header and occupying about 42-52% of canvas width.
- Body right: place a tall bottom, dress, or skirt on the right half, lower than the hero garment and occupying about 30-38% of canvas width.
- Accessories: place eyewear or small jewelry near the upper-right body area, handbag beneath the hero garment, shoes near the lower-left edge, and smaller pieces in gaps.
- Captions: position short labels beside or beneath products; keep them readable, minimal, and not in boxes.
- Footer: lower-left small uppercase archive/edit label with a short gold rule; lower-right small uppercase curator/material-board label.

## Styling Heuristics

- Keep the palette controlled: one anchor family, several neutrals, and one restrained accent.
- Repeat colors across categories so the outfit feels intentional, such as hat matching trousers or socks echoing an accent.
- Mix at least two textures, such as knit plus twill, denim plus leather, nylon plus suede, or wool plus metal.
- Include practical fashion-commerce categories: outerwear, inner/top, bottom, shoes, bag, hat/headwear, socks/legwear, jewelry/watch/eyewear.
- Prefer timeless, easy-to-understand silhouettes over abstract fashion objects.
- The board can include luxury-inspired brand/category captions and prices when the user requests them, but the products themselves should not display readable logos.

## Negative Constraints

Never include visible product logos, watermark text, price tags attached to products, hang tags, retail boxes, lifestyle props, plants, furniture, food, cosmetics, phones, colored backgrounds, or background decoration unless the user explicitly requests them. Do include intentional editorial text that belongs to the layout: title, subtitle, LOOK number, descriptor lines, product captions, optional price lines, and footer labels.
